# CULTURAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR SPAIN

## Social Behavior
- Spaniards are generally warm, friendly, and expressive in conversation
- Personal space is smaller than in Northern Europe; close physical proximity is normal
- Greetings often involve two kisses on the cheeks (right cheek first) among friends and family
- Direct eye contact is important and shows confidence and honesty
- Speaking loudly and passionately is common and not considered rude

## Daily Life & Schedule
- Late meal times: lunch around 2-3 PM, dinner around 9-10 PM or later
- Siesta culture still exists in smaller towns (businesses close 2-5 PM)
- Social life happens late: going out at 11 PM-midnight is normal
- Sunday family lunches are a sacred tradition in many households

## Communication Style
- Interrupting in conversation is normal and shows engagement, not rudeness
- Spaniards value directness but maintain politeness
- Using hands and gestures while speaking is very common
- Complaining about politics, economy, or weather is a normal conversation starter

## Work Culture
- Work-life balance is highly valued
- Long lunch breaks (1.5-2 hours) are still common in many places
- August is vacation month - many businesses close
- Personal relationships at work are important

## Social Etiquette
- Always greet shopkeepers when entering a store ("Hola, buenos días")
- Splitting bills ("ir a medias") is becoming more common among younger people
- Tipping is not mandatory but appreciated (5-10% in restaurants)
- Dress tends to be more formal/stylish than in Anglo countries

## Food & Drink
- Coffee culture is huge: café con leche in morning, café solo after meals
- Tapas culture: eating standing at bars is normal
- Wine or beer with meals is common and socially acceptable
- Never rush a meal - dining is a social experience

## Regional Differences
- Strong regional identities exist (Catalonia, Basque Country, Galicia, Andalusia)
- Be aware of language sensitivities (Catalan, Basque, Galician speakers)
- Avoid discussing regional independence issues unless you know the person well
- Each region has distinct traditions, food, and customs

## Things to Avoid
- Don't compare Spain to Latin American countries
- Don't assume everyone likes bullfighting (it's controversial)
- Don't be overly punctual for social events (15-30 min late is normal)
- Don't discuss the Civil War or Franco era unless you know the person very well
