# CULTURAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR GREECE

## Social Behavior
- Greeks are warm, expressive, and value personal relationships highly
- Physical touch is common - hugging, kissing on both cheeks, arm touching during conversation
- Hospitality (filoxenia) is a core cultural value - refusing food/drink can be seen as rude
- Direct eye contact shows honesty and engagement
- Speaking loudly and passionately is normal, not considered aggressive
- Interrupting is part of conversation flow, shows interest

## Daily Life & Schedule
- Late meal times: lunch around 2-3 PM, dinner 9-10 PM or later
- Afternoon siesta still practiced, especially in summer (2-5 PM shops may close)
- Coffee culture is central - Greeks spend hours at cafes socializing
- Greeks go out very late - nightlife starts around midnight
- Sunday is family day - extended family gatherings are sacred

## Communication Style
- Greeks are direct and honest, even if it seems blunt
- Emotional expression is valued over stoicism
- Hand gestures are integral to communication
- Personal questions about age, salary, marriage are considered normal
- Political discussions are passionate and common, even with strangers

## Work Culture
- Personal relationships matter more than strict professionalism
- Flexible approach to time - being 15-30 minutes late is normal
- Long lunch breaks are standard
- Business meetings often happen over coffee or meals
- August is vacation month - many businesses close

## Social Etiquette
- Always greet people when entering shops, elevators, waiting rooms
- Coffee (especially Greek coffee or frappe) is a social ritual, never rushed
- Refusing hospitality (food, drink) is offensive - accept at least something
- The phrase "Sto kalo" (go with good) is said when someone leaves
- "Kali orexi" (good appetite) before meals
- Smoking is still very common, especially in social settings

## Food & Drink
- Meals are lengthy social events, never rushed
- Greek coffee is traditional; frappe and freddo cappuccino are very popular
- Ouzo, tsipouro, and wine are important social drinks
- Meze culture - small plates shared among friends
- Never split bills precisely - Greeks take turns treating each other
- Taverna culture - eating out is a major social activity

## Religious & Traditional
- Greek Orthodox Christianity is deeply embedded in culture
- Name days (celebrating saint names) often bigger than birthdays
- Easter is the most important holiday - week-long celebrations
- Making the sign of the cross when passing churches is common
- Evil eye (mati) belief is widespread - blue eye amulets common
- Many traditions and superstitions are still practiced

## Regional & Social
- Strong regional identities (Crete, Macedonia, Peloponnese, etc.)
- Athens vs. Thessaloniki rivalry in culture, football, everything
- Island culture is distinct from mainland
- Village connections remain important even for city dwellers
- Football (soccer) fanaticism - especially Olympiacos, Panathinaikos, PAOK, AEK

## Things to Avoid
- Don't compare Greece to Turkey negatively (very sensitive topic)
- Don't call it "the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia" - say "North Macedonia"
- Don't rush Greeks - it's considered rude and stressful
- Don't refuse food/drink from hosts - it insults their hospitality
- Don't be overly punctual for social events - arrive 15-30 min late
- Don't discuss the economic crisis insensitively
- Don't assume all Greeks are lazy (harmful stereotype from crisis)
- Don't ignore the kafeneio culture - it's where community happens

## Greek Characteristics
- Family is paramount - multi-generational involvement in decisions
- Greeks are proud of ancient history and heritage
- "Kefi" (joy, passion, high spirits) is a cultural value
- "Meraki" (doing something with soul, creativity, love) is important
- Greeks value education highly
- Strong sense of community and neighborhood (geitonia)
