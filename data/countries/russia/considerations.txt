# CULTURAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR RUSSIA

## Social Behavior
- Russians can appear reserved or serious in public, especially with strangers
- Smiling at strangers is uncommon and may seem suspicious
- Close friends and family receive warmth, but it takes time to build trust
- Direct communication style - Russians value honesty over politeness
- Personal questions about salary, age, marital status are more acceptable than in West
- Strong handshake with direct eye contact shows respect (men)

## Daily Life & Schedule
- Meal times: breakfast 7-9 AM, lunch 12-2 PM, dinner 6-8 PM
- Tea culture is huge - tea drinking at any time is normal
- Long dinner conversations with family are important
- Sunday family gatherings are traditional
- Many still observe Soviet-era traditions despite modern life

## Communication Style
- Directness is valued - small talk is minimal
- "No" means no - Russians don't soften refusals
- Silence in conversation is acceptable and not uncomfortable
- Complaining is a normal way to bond - about government, weather, prices
- Interrupting is generally considered rude (unlike in Spain)

## Work Culture
- Work-life balance is improving but still Soviet-influenced
- Hierarchy is important - respect for authority figures
- Personal connections (связи - svyazi) are crucial for getting things done
- Many still have side jobs or dacha work on weekends
- Lunch breaks are shorter than in Southern Europe (30-60 minutes)

## Social Etiquette
- Remove shoes when entering someone's home (always)
- Bring gifts when invited to someone's home
- Never shake hands over a threshold (bad luck)
- Sit down briefly before long journey (traveler's tradition)
- Don't whistle indoors (brings financial loss)
- Even number of flowers only for funerals (always give odd numbers)
- Men are expected to hold doors, help with coats, carry heavy bags

## Food & Drink
- Vodka culture: toasts are mandatory, first toast is solemn
- Never mix different alcohols in one evening
- Always have food (закуски - zakuski) when drinking vodka
- Tea is drunk from glass in metal holder (podstakannik)
- Homemade pickles and preserves are a point of pride
- Don't refuse offered food/drink - it's considered rude
- Mayonnaise is used on everything

## Dacha Culture
- Weekend dacha trips in summer are sacred tradition
- Growing own vegetables is both necessity and hobby
- Mushroom and berry picking are serious activities (with expert knowledge)
- Banya (sauna) at dacha is weekend ritual
- Shashlik (BBQ) is primary dacha social activity

## Soviet Legacy
- Older generations have strong nostalgia for Soviet stability
- "In Soviet times..." is a common phrase in discussions
- Victory Day (May 9th) is most sacred holiday
- Many still keep Soviet-era items and habits
- Distrust of government is normal, inherited from Soviet times
- Bureaucracy is frustrating but expected

## Regional and Ethnic Differences
- Moscow and Saint Petersburg are very different from rest of Russia
- Strong regional identities exist (Siberia, Far East, Caucasus, Urals)
- Russia is multi-ethnic - over 190 ethnic groups
- Respect for ethnic Russians and ethnic minorities varies by region
- Language sensitivities exist in ethnic republics

## Things to Avoid
- Don't compare Russia to Soviet Union as if they're the same
- Don't assume everyone loves or hates Putin (very divided topic)
- Don't criticize Russia to Russians unless you know them very well
- Don't discuss Chechnya wars casually
- Don't bring up 1990s crisis unless in appropriate context
- Don't assume all Russians drink vodka heavily
- Don't whistle indoors
- Don't shake hands over threshold
- Don't give even numbers of flowers
- Don't sit at corner of table (superstition - won't marry)
- Don't put empty bottles on table (bad luck)

## Orthodox Christianity
- Russian Orthodox Church has significant cultural influence
- Even non-religious people cross themselves passing churches
- Easter and Christmas (Jan 7th) are important cultural holidays
- Icon corners in homes are still common among older generation
- Monastery and church visits are tourist activities and spiritual practice
